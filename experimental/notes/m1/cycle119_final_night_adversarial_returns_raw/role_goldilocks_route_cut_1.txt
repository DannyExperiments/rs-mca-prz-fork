## 4. Remaining official gates

Even after the finite packet is supplied and verified, the following governing clauses are still missing:

1. **Allowed field row.** The rules must permit the exact prime field
   (\mathbb F_{2^{64}-2^{32}+1}). Rejection of extension fields does not logically imply acceptance of every prime field.

2. **Challenge sampler.** The rules must state that the challenge scalar is sampled uniformly from all of (\mathbb F_q). The denominator (q) cannot be substituted for an undefined (q_{\mathrm{chal}}).

3. **Challenge-size restriction.** The rules must not impose (q_{\mathrm{chal}}\ge2^{128}), a fixed challenge field, or a separate 128-bit challenge domain. The fact that one atom of a 64-bit field already has probability above (2^{-128}) makes this clause decisive.

4. **Predicate identity.** The official bad event must be the source support-wise MCA event: the support and nearby codeword may depend on (\gamma), while the affine line (f+\gamma g) remains fixed. A rule asking for ordinary list decoding would not be satisfied by the slope packet.

5. **Protocol semantics.** A support-wise finite witness does not automatically prove soundness failure for an interactive protocol. The commitment order, dependence of witnesses on (\gamma), and verifier predicate must coincide with the source theorem.

6. **Endpoint convention.** The rules must accept distance (248) for the strict (<250) target and must not require an exact distance or exact support size different from the supplied row.

7. **(\beta=0) admissibility.** Algebraically (0\notin H), so (\beta=0) is valid for (H\subset\mathbb F_q^\times). The official format must nevertheless permit this auxiliary point if (\beta) is part of the submitted construction.

The decisive missing official clause is:

[
\boxed{\gamma\leftarrow\operatorname{Unif}(\mathbb F_q)
\text{ for this permitted Goldilocks row, with the official predicate equal to source }LD_{\mathrm{sw}}.}
]

Without it, (64/q) is not an official-prize probability calculation.

## 5. Public-fallback assessment

A fully verified direct 64-slope Goldilocks packet would be a substantially cleaner public fallback than the extension-field Cycle84 route:

* it uses a prime field with a completely smooth multiplicative-group order;
* the subgroup can be specified and checked by two exponentiations;
* the probability margin is (>2^{70});
* the claimed (7.36\times10^{16}) existential count can be omitted;
* the proof can be reduced to 64 direct polynomial-agreement checks.

But that is only the salvaged route. As supplied, the packet is absent, the huge existential bound is unproved, and the stated locator theorem would collapse all (\beta=0) slopes to one. Therefore the current fallback is not bankable.
