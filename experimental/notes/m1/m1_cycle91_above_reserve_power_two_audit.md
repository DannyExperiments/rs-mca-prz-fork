## Bankable Core Lemma

`F_q^*` contains an order-256 subgroup `H`, and since `ord_256(5)=64`,
`H` is generated by a primitive 256th root whose degree over `F_5` is 64.

At rate `rho=1/16`, `k=16`, and `ell=k+1=17`, use the canonical monomial line:

```text
f(x) = x^17
g(x) = x^16
u_z(x) = f(x) + z g(x)
```

For each 17-subset `A <= H`, the monic locator

```text
L_A(X) = prod_{a in A} (X-a)
```

has the form:

```text
L_A(X) = X^17 - (sum_{a in A} a) X^16 + R_A(X),
deg R_A < 16.
```

Thus for `z = -sum(A)`, `u_z` agrees on `A` with the degree-`<16`
codeword `-R_A`. Noncontainment holds because `x^16` cannot agree with any
degree-`<16` polynomial on 17 points.

Therefore every distinct 17-subset sum contributes a noncontained MCA-bad
slope. The power-basis/sign-digit construction gives at least

```text

```

distinct subset sums, already far above `floor(q/2^128)`.
