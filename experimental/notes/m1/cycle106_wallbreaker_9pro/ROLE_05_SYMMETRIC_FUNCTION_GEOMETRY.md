# Role 05: Symmetric-Function Geometry of M_s

Your role is to analyze the geometry of the layers:

```text
M_s = {((-1)^l e_l(Sbar))_{l=1}^{sigma+1}: Sbar subset mu_n, |Sbar|=s}
```

as structured finite subsets/varieties in coefficient space, and to bound
their intersection with the curve:

```text
Gamma = {(g_1(theta),...,g_{sigma+1}(theta)): theta in F_p}.
```

Focus on the subset-size layer, complement duality, and the fact that `M_s`
comes from squarefree divisors of `1-X^n`, not arbitrary coefficients.

Try to prove one of:

1. `Gamma` is not contained in any positive-dimensional component generated by
   the closure of `M_s`, except under a forbidden periodic/aperiodic failure.
2. Any large intersection forces a low-degree algebraic relation among the
   `g_l(theta)` that contradicts above-reserve aperiodicity.
3. The layer geometry admits a uniform degree/Bezout-style bound independent
   of `s`.

If the Zariski closure of `M_s` is too large to bound anything, state that
precisely as a `ROUTE_CUT` and identify the corrected finite/discrete property
that must replace closure.

Return `PROOF`, `BANKABLE_LEMMA`, `ROUTE_CUT`, `EXACT_NEW_WALL`, or `PLAN`.
