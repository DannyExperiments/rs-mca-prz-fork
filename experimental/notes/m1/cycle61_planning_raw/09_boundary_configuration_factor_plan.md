# Executive verdict

**No—the current route, as presently organized, is not a plausible closing route.** It is a productive counterexample-discovery program, but it is not yet a convergent proof program.

The project is **still missing hard inverse theorems**, not merely finite bookkeeping or one technical lemma:

1. a scalar full-support locator-fiber inverse theorem for the list challenge;
2. an all-line syndrome transverse-secant inverse theorem for MCA;
3. a finite hereditary-envelope aggregation theorem;
4. a genuinely prize-strength primitive mixing theorem.

The quotient/container explosion is evidence that the theorem is being stated at the wrong level. Genus-zero maps, Lattès maps, point-fiber quotients, divisor norms, configuration characters, affine envelopes, and H2 defects should not each become independent terminal terms. They are manifestations of anomalously large fibers of one locator-configuration map.

My assessment:

* **Current exception-ledger route is non-closing:** high confidence, (0.95).
* **A boundary-localized, configuration-factor reformulation is the best repair:** moderate-high confidence, (0.75).
* **Both grand challenges solved in the next two theorem-worker rounds:** low confidence, (0.15).
* **A decisive route reduction or a genuine new inverse lemma in two rounds:** moderate confidence, (0.65).
* **List challenge:** medium-to-long range.
* **MCA challenge:** longer range; solving the scalar theorem only settles the (t=1) core, not arbitrary syndrome lines.

The route is mathematically plausible, but it is not close.

# AUDIT

## 1. The project is solving a harder uniform classification problem than the prize asks

The official objects are finite and code-specific. For

[
T_q=\left\lfloor\frac q{2^{128}}\right\rfloor,
]

the only reserves that matter are those near the first safe reserve

[
\widehat\sigma_{\rm MCA}(C)
===========================

\min{\sigma:M_C(\sigma)\le T_q},
]

and analogously for the scalar list numerator (L_C(\sigma)).

### PROOF — frontier relevance lemma

Both bad-slope and list numerators are nonincreasing as the reserve increases. Therefore:

> A counterpacket occurring only at a reserve already known to be unsafe cannot move (\delta_C^*). It may refute an overgeneral upper theorem, but it does not justify expanding the prize theorem unless it persists at or above the unresolved frontier.

This elementary point has not been enforced strongly enough.

Applied to Cycle 60:

* The degree-31 Lattès packet has (n=8192), (\sigma=31), rate (1/4). The banked field-uniform quotient floor is (n/256=32). It is below the existing unsafe frontier.
* The degree-32 companion lies at that floor, where the known multiplicative quotient profile is already unsafe.
* The degree-113 Lattès packet has (n=65536), (\sigma=113), rate (1/16). The known floor is (n/512=128). Again it is below the existing frontier.

Thus the supplied Lattès packets are presently **taxonomy counterexamples**, not threshold-moving prize counterexamples. They should be verified, but they should not dictate the master architecture unless an instance is produced above all simpler certified failure floors.

By contrast, the projective-subline packet at rate (1/4),

[
n=2^{19},\qquad \sigma=n/32,
]

is genuinely frontier-relevant: its claimed (28{,}048{,}800) slopes exceed (T_q=16{,}776{,}960), while the old monomial profiles, tangent term, occupancy term, and proper-envelope tests all clear. This packet is the highest-value verification target.

## 2. The divisor-product mechanism is relevant only in code-dependent regions

The raw finite example in `05_divisor_norm_character_counterpacket.md` is not at an exact official rate. That alone prevents its numerical instance from entering the final prize ledger.

The general construction is more important than the example, however, and can be shifted to official dimensions. Its approximate support scale is

[
\frac{\binom n{k+\sigma}}{nq^{\sigma-2}},
]

which is a factor (q/n) above the ordinary occupancy scale.

At a field-uniform quotient floor (\eta_0=\sigma/n), the leading exponential comparison is

[
\log_2 \mathsf{PC}
------------------

# \log_2 T_q

n\bigl(H_2(\rho+\eta_0)-\eta_0\log_2q\bigr)
+O(\log q+\log n).
]

The corresponding asymptotic crossover bitlengths are:

| Rate   | Existing floor | Crossover (\log_2 q) |
| ------ | -------------: | -------------------: |
| (1/2)  |        (1/256) |            (255.989) |
| (1/4)  |        (1/256) |            (209.257) |
| (1/8)  |        (1/256) |            (141.934) |
| (1/16) |        (1/512) |            (176.576) |

Consequences:

* At rate (1/2), the product-character mechanism can remain relevant almost all the way to the (2^{256}) field cap.
* At rates (1/4,1/8,1/16), it is dominated by the known quotient floor in sufficiently large fields, but may matter in the lower-bit field range.
* The exact finite corrections near the crossover must be computed with integer or interval arithmetic.

So Cycle 60 drew too broad a conclusion in one direction, but dismissing configuration characters entirely would also be wrong. They require a **frontier relevance gate**, not automatic inclusion or automatic exclusion.

## 3. The genus ledger is not the canonical theorem level

Classifying rational functions by the genus of their Galois closure can organize point-fiber quotient examples. It cannot be the terminal inverse theorem because:

* divisor-product conditions live on (\operatorname{Sym}^j(L)), not on individual points;
* several independent configuration characters can coexist;
* in the (t=1) apolar normal form, generator shears change a selected ratio (B/A), so that ratio is not canonical;
* the same support family may admit several unrelated rational-map descriptions;
* “all maximal rich split-rational systems” is not a finite registry until maximality, equivalence, and complexity are defined intrinsically.

The correct object is the large fiber of a **locator-configuration map**, not its chosen parametrization by a rational map.

## 4. The proposed high-genus discrepancy branch is numerically inadequate as stated

### PROOF — square-root barrier

For every officially allowed field (q<2^{256}),

[
\sqrt q>\frac q{2^{128}}.
]

Therefore any terminal primitive term of the form

[
C\sqrt q,\qquad C\ge1,
]

already exceeds the prize budget. Equality is possible only at (q=2^{256}), which is outside the strict field cap.

Hence a standard Weil or Frobenius (O(\sqrt q)) estimate cannot close the high-genus branch. It may help classify exceptional maps, but it cannot be the final charge. The primitive theorem must exploit multiple independent conditions and produce occupancy-scale or stronger-than-square-root cancellation.

This is a hard numerical route cut against

[
\texttt{W-SRQ-HIGH-GENUS-FROBENIUS-DISCREPANCY-CONTAINER}
]

unless that wall is reformulated with an explicit sub-square-root prize bound.

## 5. Several current “reductions” do not reduce the hard problem

* The critical-seed completion theorem collapses to the original colored syndrome fiber once coefficient vectors are included.
* The scalar circuit transversal theorem is exact and useful, but a hitting set for all circuits can be as hard as bounding the original list.
* H2-primitivity is presently defined by declaring H2-defective objects exceptional. Without a global charge for those objects, it is a diagnostic, not an upper theorem.
* “Primitive” currently means “not yet assigned to another container.” That is not a mathematical property.
* The fixed-partition QAR theorem is bankable only in its aligned form (M\mid k) with a reserve-sized defect. It does not establish a varying-defect or global-registry theorem.

## 6. The list and MCA challenges do not collapse to one theorem

The scalar full-support theorem is common infrastructure:

* it is the entire interleaved-list challenge after the banked projection theorem;
* it is essentially the (t=1) JR/MCA core, with the dimension shifted by one and an external color.

But it does **not** imply an arbitrary-line MCA upper bound. There is no banked reduction from general denominator-free syndrome pencils to (t=1), and the attempted (t>\sigma) compression route has failed.

# Repaired formulation

The most useful intrinsic formulation is the restricted subset-product geometry of locators.

In a JR chart with squarefree denominator (E), put

[
A_E=F[X]/(E),\qquad \xi_x=[X-x]\in A_E^\times.
]

For an error support (T),

[
[L_T]*E=\prod*{x\in T}\xi_x.
]

The support-fiber problem is therefore a restricted subset-product problem for the structured atom set

[
{\xi_x:x\in L}\subset A_E^\times,
]

supplemented by linear jet constraints and a color functional.

This single object contains:

* monomial and projective point quotients;
* norm-one and subgroup quotients;
* divisor norms (\prod_{x\in T}R(x));
* several simultaneous configuration characters;
* stabilizers created by Lattès or other group actions;
* the ordinary occupancy term when the atom products mix.

A point-fiber quotient and a divisor-product character are not fundamentally different theorem classes. They are different low-complexity factors of the same locator-configuration distribution.

For arbitrary MCA lines, the analogue should be defined through the Fitting ideals or invariant factors of the line’s universal locator module, not through a chosen denominator or chosen ratio (B/A).

## Grand MCA challenge

### Logical minimum

For each code (C), it suffices to prove:

1. **MCA upper certificate.** Identify an integer (\widehat\sigma) and prove
   [
   M_C(\widehat\sigma)\le T_q.

   [
   M_C(\widehat\sigma-1)>T_q.

3. **Exact monotonicity and endpoint conversion.**

Then the largest safe grid radius is

[
1-\rho-\frac{\widehat\sigma}{n}.
]

With the repository’s integer-radius convention, the real supremum is shifted by one grid interval:

[
1-\rho-\frac{\widehat\sigma-1}{n},

with the endpoint itself unsafe when the lower certificate is strict.

The upper certificate currently decomposes into four genuinely missing results:

1. **Hereditary-envelope mass theorem**
   [

2. **Configuration-factor extraction theorem**
   [
   \text{large packet}\Longrightarrow

   ]

   [
   \sum_{\text{minimal factors }\Xi}

4. **Primitive mixing theorem**
   [
   D_{\rm prim}(C,\sigma)

The final exact ledger must prove
