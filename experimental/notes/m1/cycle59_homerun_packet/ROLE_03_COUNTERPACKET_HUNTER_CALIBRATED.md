# ROLE 03 - Counterpacket Hunter Against The Calibrated Theorem

Try to kill the repaired theorem, not the already-dead pure `n^{1+o(1)}` target.

Construct, if possible, a same-field smooth-domain RS example and one syndrome line / residue datum with more bad slopes than

```text
n^{1+o(1)}
+ O(binom(n,k+sigma)/q^{sigma-1})
+ explicit quotient/action-rank contribution
+ tangent/common-envelope contribution.
```

Rules:

1. You may not count a packet that is just a literal quotient pullback, quotient-action-rank low packet, fixed-defect quotient packet, tangent/common-core packet, same-witness support inflation, or contained witness.
2. If your construction uses quotient structure, compute `d_M(E)=deg minpoly([X^M] mod E)` and show whether the quotient term should charge it.
3. If your construction is high-denominator `t>sigma`, translate it into syndrome transverse-secant language and show it is not a chart artifact.
4. Track exact slope count, field size, domain smoothness, rate, radius, and finite `2^-128` relevance.

A valid counterpacket should force a route-board change. A failed attack is still useful if you isolate the obstruction as a BANKABLE_LEMMA.
