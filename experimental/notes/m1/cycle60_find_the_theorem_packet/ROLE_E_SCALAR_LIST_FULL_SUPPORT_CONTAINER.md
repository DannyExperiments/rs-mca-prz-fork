# ROLE E - Scalar List Full-Support Container

Find the scalar list theorem that would solve the grand list challenge after
projection.

For `C=RS[F,L,k]`, a received word `y`, and a codeword `P`, define the full
agreement support

```text
A(P) = {x in L : P(x)=y(x)}.
```

Do not use raw feasible support fibers.

Find a finite theorem bounding

```text
max_s sum_{e=0}^{r-sigma} nu_e^circ(s)
```

or equivalently the number of actual scalar codewords with agreement at least
`k+sigma`, after charging split-rational quotient packets and overagreement /
common-core containers.

Prove the exact projection step to interleaved lists at `epsilon*=2^-128`, then
state exactly what scalar finite bound remains.

If false, give an actual-list counterpacket with distinct codewords and full
supports, not merely many feasible support subsets.
