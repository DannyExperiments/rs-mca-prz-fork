# ROLE A - Split-Rational Quotient Classifier

Find the theorem behind the new quotient ledger.

Classify rational maps

```text
R(X)=P(X)/Q(X)
```

of degree `M` whose fibers split heavily inside a smooth RS evaluation domain
`L=alpha H`.

Target theorem:

If `R` has enough disjoint full `M`-point fibers inside `L` to support a
superlinear MCA or scalar-list packet, then `R` is generated by one of the
allowed quotient symmetries:

```text
monomial X^d,
dihedral/inversion quotient,
projective-subline quotient,
norm-one/unit-circle quotient,
or another explicit finite exceptional family.
```

Then packets are charged by

```text
d_R(E)=deg minpoly([R]_E)
```

and the corresponding full support profile.

If false, construct a nonmonomial split-rational quotient on an official
power-of-two smooth domain that beats the proposed finite ledger.

Be precise about projective automorphisms of RS codes and about which domains
have exceptional `PGL_2` stabilizers.
