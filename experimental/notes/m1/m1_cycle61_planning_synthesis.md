**Date:** 2026-06-20

## Executive Verdict

All nine planning answers agree on the central point: the full Proximity Prize
problem is **not close**. The project has strong lower/failure machinery,
exact reformulations, and several useful route repairs, but it lacks the
safe-side inverse/container theorems needed to certify exact prize radii.

The planning round is still valuable. It converges on a route correction:
stop expanding an exception ledger one named family at a time, and instead
formulate canonical finite objects and structure-or-discrepancy theorems at
the support/configuration level.

## Consensus

- All nine say the full prize problem is not close.
- All reject a rate-only finite answer. The exact threshold depends on the
  code, domain, fields, divisibility, active block systems, configuration
  packets, and integer rounding.
- All agree the lower/failure side is much stronger than the safe-side upper.
- Most agree the exception-ledger route is expanding too much and must be
  replaced by a canonical structure/container theorem.
- Most prioritize exact finite objects and support-level formulations over
  denominator/genus taxonomy.
- The denominator-free syndrome transverse-secant language remains the best
  global MCA formulation.
- The interleaved list branch is no longer the main wall at the official
  same-field scale; the scalar full-support list theorem is the remaining
  wall.

## Main Candidate Next Walls

The answers propose several overlapping next-wall names:

```text
W-LIST-APOLAR-MINIMAL-GENERATOR-INVERSE
W-JR-T1-GJ-QUOTIENT-CONDITIONED-SUBSET-PRODUCT-LOCAL-LIMIT
W-JR-T1-DENSITY-TO-WEIGHTED-SUPPORT-MODULE-CONTAINER
W-PRIZE-BOUNDARY-CONFIGURATION-FACTOR-EXTRACTION
W-MCA-SUPPORT-MONODROMY-WEIGHTED-OVERLAP-COVER
W-MCA-TWO-BLOCK-SYSTEM-OVERLAP
W-LIST-APOLAR-CI-SPLIT-DIVISOR-LOCAL-LIMIT
```

These are not nine different programs. They cluster into four real tasks:

1. **Scalar/list apolar base theorem.** Prove a finite local-limit or inverse
   theorem for fully split divisors/forms inside one apolar complete
   intersection, modulo block/core/envelope structure.
2. **MCA support-overlap theorem.** Prove that rich split-rational or
   support-block systems realized on one envelope-free syndrome line have
   bounded weighted overlap, or force a proper envelope.
3. **Primitive `t=1` configuration theorem.** Prove or kill a boundary-local
   `t=1` structure-or-occupancy theorem, incorporating product/divisor-norm
   characters and generalized-Jacobian/subset-product structure where needed.
4. **Finite certificate layer.** Freeze threshold conventions and build exact
   integer checkers before accepting asymptotic statements as prize-relevant.

## Conflicts

- Some plans say scalar list is healthier and should be attacked first; others
  say MCA support overlap is the central wall.
- Some treat Lattes verification as high priority; others say genus taxonomy
  is secondary unless it affects support overlap.
- Some want generalized Jacobian/subset-product formalism; others want apolar
  complete-intersection or configuration-factor extraction.
- Some view the scalar list circuit theorem as the clean next wall; others
  think circuit transversals are the wrong abstraction and should be replaced
  by apolar split-divisor local limits.
- Some plans emphasize verifying finite counterpackets first; others argue the
  route should first formalize the exact finite spine and only then verify
  packets against written statements.

## What Is Actually Actionable

The actionable work is narrower than the number of wall names suggests.

1. **Verify Lattes arithmetic.**
   - Degree-31 packet: curve order, kernel, fiber counts, slope count,
     noncontainment, no-envelope, and field/domain admissibility.
   - Degree-113 packet: CM sign, subgroup/kernel arithmetic, pole-free
     normalization, fiber counts, and official constraints.

2. **Formalize exact finite objects.**
   - Exact MCA numerator `M_C(sigma)`.
   - Exact scalar list numerator.
   - Threshold convention `T_q=floor(q/2^128)`.
   - Endpoint/radius convention.
   - Generated-field versus line-field versus challenge-field ledger.

3. **Build finite checker skeleton.**
   - Input: `(n,k,q_gen,q_line,q_chal,sigma,domain descriptors, quotient terms,
     envelope/tangent floors, primitive allowance, lower certificate)`.
   - Output: exact safe/fail/unknown at each reserve.
   - Use integer arithmetic; floating logs only for exploration.

4. **Write small enumerators.**
   - `t=1` apolar split-numerator enumerator for small fields/domains.
   - Scalar full-support circuit enumerator and minimum transversal checker.
   - Lattes/projective packet verification scripts.

5. **Prove or kill the first restricted boundary-local theorem.**
   - Restrict to exact official rates where possible.
   - Start with `t=1`.
   - Start envelope-free.
   - Start with one saved condition / corank one.
   - Do not attempt all genus/all `t`/all hereditary descendants first.

## Wishful Or Too Broad

- “Complete genus-zero/genus-one/high-genus registry” as a primary route.
- Undefined primitive discrepancy theorems.
- Any `n^{1+o(1)}` statement as a finite prize certificate.
- “Classify all quotient mechanisms” without a support-overlap theorem.
- Broad all-`t` MCA inverse before the `t=1` or boundary-local base case is
  resolved.
- Treating circuit transversals as automatically easier than the original
  scalar list problem.
- Treating Lattes/genus taxonomy as sufficient without a collective
  line-level overlap or weighted-cover theorem.

## Independent Signals Across The Nine Answers

### Strong repeated signals

- The current exception-ledger route is not converging by enumeration.
- The scalar list branch has the cleanest narrowed target: apolar
  split-divisor/local-limit language.
- The MCA branch is harder because it is dynamic: one syndrome line/pencil,
  hereditary envelopes, and all denominator presentations.
- The finite ledger must be built early because many asymptotic-looking terms
  are not prize certificates.

### More contentious signals

- Whether the next primary push should be scalar list first or MCA
  support-overlap first.
- Whether product/divisor-norm characters should be a separate ledger term or
  a forced resultant/subresultant phenomenon inside the apolar theorem.
- Whether Lattes verification is a first-priority blocker or a secondary
  sanity check after support-level theorem formalization.

## Recommended Master-Referee Decision Problem

The final master-referee instance should not brainstorm. It should choose
between these priorities:

```text
A. verify Lattes / split-rational registry first;
B. prove support-theoretic split-rational overlap cover first;
C. attack primitive t=1 apolar / generalized-Jacobian base case first;
D. attack scalar list apolar/circuit theorem first;
E. build finite checker and threshold ledger first.
```

It should output one primary route, one backup route, a two-round worker
allocation, and explicit stop conditions.

## Provisional Recommendation Before Master Referee

The strongest planning consensus points toward this sequence:

1. **Freeze the finite objects and checker skeleton.**
   This prevents another round from producing theorem statements that cannot
   certify `2^-128`.

2. **Verify the degree-31 Lattes packet and the projective equality-scale
   packet.**
   These are route-shaping kill tests. The degree-113 packet can wait unless
   degree 31 fails.

3. **Attack the scalar/list apolar base wall.**
   It is the cleanest narrowed mathematical target and may also clarify the
   `t=1` MCA/JR branch.

4. **In parallel, formulate the MCA support-overlap theorem.**
   Do not try to solve the whole MCA safe-side theorem until the support
   overlap object is precise.

This is not a full solve plan yet. It is the best route-narrowing plan to hand
to one final master planner.
