## 1. Exact point-mass reduction

For a source prefix profile, let

\[
\Omega^0={T\choose a},\qquad M=|\Omega^0|,\qquad
\Phi:\Omega^0\to G,
\]

and let

\[
L=|\Phi(\Omega^0)|.
\]

Suppose that an actual semantically surviving exact-agreement family

\[
\mathcal F\subseteq\Phi^{-1}(s_0)
\]

has size \(W\).  Put

\[
f=W1_{\{s_0\}}.
\]

This `f` is the count function of a source-fiber submask, not necessarily the
count function of the whole first-match residual. The reduction below is
therefore an input to a *hereditary* source-algebraic emission theorem, unless the atlas separately
proves that every such fiber restriction is itself an admissible rooted
profile with the original full-slice normalization. Ordinary source-algebraic emission on the
whole residual does not automatically apply to this submask.

Let the nonzero dual be partitioned into the complete bands used in Steps
1--8.  Since \(|\tau(\gamma)|\le N\), the number of bands is at most

\[
K_N=2+\lceil\log_2N\rceil.
\]

Hence one complete nonzero band \(A\) satisfies

\[
\delta_A=\frac{|A|}{|G|}
\ge \frac{|G|-1}{|G|K_N}
\ge \frac1{2K_N}
\]

when \(|G|\ge2\).

### Proposition 1.1 (point-mass band lower bound)

For that band,

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal R_A(f)
\ge
\frac{WL}{M}\,
\delta_A\,L^{-1/q}.
}
\]

#### Proof

For every \(\gamma\),

\[
\widehat f(\gamma)=W\overline{\gamma(s_0)}.
\]

Consequently,

\[
P_Af(s_0)
=
\frac1{|G|}
\sum_{\gamma\in A}
W\overline{\gamma(s_0)}\gamma(s_0)
=
W\delta_A.
\]

Therefore

\[
\|P_Af\|_q\ge W\delta_A,
\]

and

\[
\frac{L^{1-1/q}}M\|P_Af\|_q
\ge
\frac{WL}{M}\delta_A L^{-1/q}.
\qquad\square
\]

### Corollary 1.2 (overloaded source fiber produces a complete-band restriction failure)

If, for a fixed \(\eta>0\),

\[
\frac{WL}{M}\ge e^{2\eta N},
\qquad
\frac{\log L}{q}=o(N),
\]

then, for all sufficiently large \(N\),

\[
\boxed{\mathcal R_A(f)\ge e^{\eta N}.}
\]

Indeed,

\[
\log\mathcal R_A(f)
\ge
2\eta N-\log(2K_N)-\frac{\log L}{q}
=
2\eta N-o(N).
\]

This is an actual band failure, not merely a high-\(\mathcal Y_A\) tag.

## 4. Universal annihilators do not imply source foldings

The owner-rooted quotient packet is generated by

\[
u=\Phi(S_0)-\Phi(S_1),\qquad K=\langle u\rangle.
\]

The following exact prime-field example shows that this datum need not identify
any source-coordinate folding modulo \(K\).

Take

\[
\mathbb B=\mathbb F_7,\qquad
T=\mathbb F_7,\qquad
v_t=(t,t^2,t^3)\in\mathbb F_7^3.
\]

Let

\[
S_0=\{0,1\},\qquad S_1=\{2,3\}.
\]

Then

\[
u=\sum_{t\in S_0}v_t-\sum_{t\in S_1}v_t=(3,2,1).
\]

### Proposition 4.1 (no nontrivial column folding modulo the rooted secant)

For all distinct \(x,y\in\mathbb F_7\),

\[
v_x-v_y\notin\langle u\rangle.
\]

Hence the quotient map

\[
\pi:\mathbb F_7^3\to\mathbb F_7^3/\langle u\rangle
\]

is injective on the source columns \(\{v_t:t\in T\}\).  In particular, the
cyclic annihilator packet does not produce a nontrivial coordinate folding.

#### Proof

Suppose

\[
v_x-v_y=c(3,2,1)
\]

with \(x\ne y\).  The first two coordinates give

\[
x-y=3c,\qquad
(x-y)(x+y)=2c,
\]

so \(x+y=2/3=3\) in \(\mathbb F_7\).  The third coordinate gives

\[
(x-y)(x^2+xy+y^2)=c,
\]

hence

\[
x^2+xy+y^2=1/3=5.
\]

Using \(x+y=3\),

\[
x^2+xy+y^2=(x+y)^2-xy=2-xy,
\]

so \(xy=4\).  The polynomial with roots \(x,y\) is

\[
X^2-3X+4,
\]

whose discriminant is \(9-16=0\) in \(\mathbb F_7\).  Thus \(x=y\), a
contradiction. \(\square\)

This same example has no proper subfield.  It therefore also proves that the
universal secant construction contains no automatic field-descent datum.

It does not rule out every atlas notion of quotient. It is specifically a
regression against treating an arbitrary cyclic annihilator as an automatic
coordinate folding or proper-field descent.

The example is a guardrail about the owner-rooted object; it is not claimed to be an
complete-band restriction bound falsifier.

## 5. Why the proposed routes stop

### 5.1 Trade/locator route

For a fixed root \(S_0\), a radius-\(r\) trade is encoded by

\[
I=S_0\setminus S,\qquad
J=S\setminus S_0,\qquad |I|=|J|=r.
\]

In the balanced regime \(r=\beta N\), the number of possible moving-side
pairs is

\[
\binom a r\binom{N-a}r
=
\exp\!\left(
N\left[
\alpha h\!\left(\frac\beta\alpha\right)
+(1-\alpha)h\!\left(\frac\beta{1-\alpha}\right)
\right]+o(N)
\right).
\]

An \(e^{2\eta N}\)-sized trade family may therefore occupy exponentially many
unrelated cores and side pairs.  Cardinality and rim packing alone do not
yield an \(e^{o(N)}\)-template census.  A common divisor, locator pencil, or
repeated side pattern requires an additional source inverse theorem.

### 5.2 Quotient-coset route

Restriction to a \(K^\perp\)-coset says only that selected characters have one
fixed value on one selected secant.  Proposition 4.1 shows that this need not
create any nontrivial folding of the moment columns.  A slope-projection
budget is also absent.

### 5.3 Field route

A prime-field profile has no proper subfield.  A prime-line
\(\langle u\rangle_{\mathbb F_p}\) or a trace hyperplane is therefore not a
proper-field descent certificate.

### 5.4 Rank route

Each equation

\[
U_{o(S)}|_S=h_S|_S
\]

belongs to the generic witness locus.  Direct sums of those interpolation
matrices have only baseline rank loss.  Neither equal-syndrome trades nor a
single spectral annihilator supplies an additional common minor.

### 5.5 Energy/BSG route

The flat packet permits projected energy to be essentially diagonal.  In that
case ordinary additive energy supplies no approximate-group input.  A
low-energy heavy fiber consists of many independent choices at one syndrome,
not repeated differences.

### 5.6 Bohr and polynomial-phase routes

A Bohr set or a trace hyperplane gives approximate or spectral phase
concentration.  It does not by itself produce an exact folding, common locator
factor, Frobenius descent, extra common minor, or saturation incidence.
Converting it to one of those outputs is exactly another source inverse
theorem.

## 6. Correct theorem interface

The five algebraic alternatives are not an exhaustive first-match ledger.
A nonstructured packet needs an analytic signed-minor alternative.

A precise replacement is the following.

> **Charge-preserving semantic-or-signed dichotomy.**
> For every actual positive-rooted failure packet, decompose its exact positive
> correlation into at most \(e^{o(N)}\) rooted packets with printed charges
> \[
> (B_i,\mathcal U_i,c_i).
> \]
> The decomposition is charge-preserving:
> \[
> c_i\ge0,
> \qquad
> \sum_i c_i=\Omega_+,
> \qquad
> c_i\le\sum_{S\in\mathcal U_i}\omega(S),
> \qquad
> \|P_{B_i}b_{\mathcal U_i}\|_q\ge c_i.
> \]
> Each packet is either:
>
> 1. one of the five source-semantic precursors, with its actual owner and
>    verifier-checkable parameters; or
> 2. a signed-minor packet whose assigned charge satisfies
>    \[
>    c_i
>    \le
>    e^{o(N)}
>    \frac{M}{L^{1-1/q}}.
>    \]
>
> If the charges are defined by signed character correlations, their
> nonnegativity and exact sum are part of the theorem, not inferred from an
> overlap census. Character and support multiplicities must still be recorded
> so that the packet census costs only \(e^{o(N)}\).

### Proposition 6.1 (the dichotomy implies source-algebraic emission on an actual failure)

If

\[
\mathcal R_A(f)\ge e^{\eta N},
\]

the total positive correlation is at least

\[
e^{\eta N}\frac{M}{L^{1-1/q}}.
\]

The sum of all signed-minor charges is at most

\[
e^{o(N)}\frac{M}{L^{1-1/q}},
\]

which is exponentially smaller. Therefore the semantic packets carry
\((1-o(1))\Omega_+\) of the assigned positive charge. Since there are only
\(e^{o(N)}\) of them, one semantic packet has charge at least

\[
e^{-o(N)}\Omega_+.
\]

The charge-preserving conditions then give both

\[
\sum_{S\in\mathcal U_i}\omega(S)
\ge e^{-o(N)}\Omega_+
\]

and

\[
\mathcal R_{B_i}(b_{\mathcal U_i})
\ge
e^{-o(N)}\mathcal R_A(f),
\]

The one-way interpolation gate gives the valid absolute lower bound

\[
\mathcal Y_{B_i}(b_{\mathcal U_i})
\ge
\mathcal R_{B_i}(b_{\mathcal U_i})^q
\ge
e^{-o(Nq)}\mathcal R_A(f)^q
\ge
e^{\eta Nq-o(Nq)}.
\]

It does **not** imply
`$\mathcal Y_{B_i}\ge e^{-o(Nq)}\mathcal Y_A$`, because the finite owner-rooting and projected-energy reductions only prove
`$\mathcal R_A^q\le\mathcal Y_A$` in the opposite direction.

Thus the dichotomy proves the desired rooted semantic conclusion. Its analytic clause
is exactly the missing signed moment-curve/minor-arc theorem; it cannot be
deleted without replacing it by a structural max-fiber inverse of equal
strength.
