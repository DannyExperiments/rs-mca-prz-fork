## 1. Input retained from owner-rooted localization

Let

\[
 A=-A\subseteq \widehat G\setminus\{0\}
\]

be the complete band from an actual dense-band failure.  Let `f` be the
actual residual count function, let `g` be its exact norming dual, and put

\[
 \omega(S)=\left[\operatorname{Re}
 \overline{(P_Ag)(\Phi(S))}\right]_+,
 \qquad
 \mathcal S_+=\{S\in\Omega^\circ:\omega(S)>0\}.
\]

Write

\[
 n_+=|\mathcal S_+|,\qquad
 \Omega_+=\sum_{S\in\mathcal S_+}\omega(S),
\]

and define the positive-support count function

\[
 b(s)=|\{S\in\mathcal S_+:\Phi(S)=s\}|.
\]

The inherited exact facts are

\[
 \Omega_+\ge \|P_Af\|_q,
 \qquad
 \|P_Ab\|_q\ge \Omega_+,
 \qquad
 |S\cap S'|\le a-2\quad(S\ne S').
 \tag{1.1}
\]

The word `quotient` below is spectral: it means restriction of characters to
the cyclic subgroup generated by one positive-positive source secant.  This is
an exact and useful localization, but it is universal once two distinct
positive syndromes exist.  It is therefore not, by itself, the nontrivial
source factorization or slope-projection estimate required by a paid quotient
atlas cell.

Assume the flat branch

\[
 \Omega_+^2<2n_+
 \tag{1.2}
\]

and the actual normalized excess

\[
 \mathcal R_A(f)
 :=\frac{L^{1-1/q}}M\|P_Af\|_q
 \ge e^{\eta N}.
 \tag{1.3}
\]

For a character packet `B`, define the positive-packet ledgers

\[
 E_B(b)=\|P_Bb\|_2^2,
 \qquad
 e_B(b)=\frac{L E_B(b)}{M^2},
\]

\[
 x_B(b)=L\frac{n_+}{M}\frac{|B|}{|G|},
 \qquad
 \mathcal Y_B(b)=e_B(b)x_B(b)^{q-2}.
 \tag{1.4}
\]

The projected-energy interpolation gate applies to `b` as well:

\[
 \mathcal R_A(b)^q\le \mathcal Y_A(b),
 \qquad
 \mathcal R_A(b)\ge\mathcal R_A(f).
 \tag{1.5}
\]

## 2. A positive-subpacket lemma

For every `U subset S_+`, let

\[
 b_U(s)=|\{S\in U:\Phi(S)=s\}|.
\]

Then

\[
 \boxed{
 \|P_A b_U\|_q
 \ge \sum_{S\in U}\omega(S).
 }
 \tag{2.1}
\]

Indeed, self-adjointness of `P_A`, the same norming dual `g`, and positivity
on `U` give

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 \sum_{S\in U}\omega(S)
 &=\operatorname{Re}\langle b_U,P_Ag\rangle\\
 &=\operatorname{Re}\langle P_Ab_U,g\rangle\\
 &\le \|P_Ab_U\|_q\|g\|_{q'}
 =\|P_Ab_U\|_q.
 \end{aligned}
\]

The flat condition is therefore quantitatively nonempty.  From (1.1)--(1.3),

\[
 n_+>\frac{\Omega_+^2}{2}
 \ge \frac{e^{2\eta N}}2
 \left(\frac{M}{L^{1-1/q}}\right)^2
 \ge \frac12 e^{2\eta N},
 \tag{2.2}
\]

because `L <= M`.

## 3. Planted branch: the positive image is a singleton

Assume

\[
 |\Phi(\mathcal S_+)|=1.
 \tag{3.1}
\]

Choose a root `S_0 in S_+`.  Since `omega(S)` depends only on `Phi(S)`, all
positive supports have one common weight `c>0`.

For `S != S_0`, put

\[
 r(S)=|S_0\setminus S|=|S\setminus S_0|.
\]

Rim packing gives `2 <= r(S) <= a`.  There are at most `a-1 <= N` possible
values.  Hence some `r` has

\[
 \mathcal T_r
 :=\{S\in\mathcal S_+\setminus\{S_0\}:r(S)=r\},
 \qquad
 |\mathcal T_r|\ge\frac{n_+-1}{a-1}.
 \tag{3.2}
\]

For every `S in T_r`, additivity and equal syndrome give the exact trade

\[
 \boxed{
 \sum_{t\in S_0\setminus S}v_t
 =\sum_{t\in S\setminus S_0}v_t,
 \qquad
 |S_0\setminus S|=|S\setminus S_0|=r.
 }
 \tag{3.3}
\]

The family (3.3) is rooted at the actual positive-weight owner `o(S_0)` and,
for each incidence, at the actual owner `o(S)`.  It has at least

\[
 \frac{e^{2\eta N}/2-1}{N}
 =e^{2\eta N-o(N)}
 \tag{3.4}
\]

incidences.  Its dual mass is

\[
 c|\mathcal T_r|
 \ge \frac{n_+-1}{n_+(a-1)}\Omega_+
 \ge \frac{\Omega_+}{2N}
 \tag{3.5}
\]

for all sufficiently large `N`.  By (2.1), the rooted core
`{S_0} union T_r` retains normalized q-excess at least

\[
 \frac1{2N}\mathcal R_A(f)=e^{\eta N-o(N)}.
 \tag{3.6}
\]

For the owner-rooted packet itself one may retain `A`, all of `S_+`, every owner,
and all positive mass; (3.2)--(3.5) are the printed planted structural datum.
Thus support, character, and owner cardinality losses are exactly one, while
the planted incidence loses only a factor `O(N)`.

The paired regression is necessarily caught here: two pair-union supports
with one exchanged complete pair produce the printed relation
`A_i+(C-A_i)=A_j+(C-A_j)`.

## 4. Quotient branch: the positive image has at least two points

Assume now

\[
 |\Phi(\mathcal S_+)|\ge2.
 \tag{4.1}
\]

Choose positive supports `S_0,S_1` with distinct syndromes, minimizing

\[
 d_*
 =\operatorname{ord}\bigl(\Phi(S_0)-\Phi(S_1)\bigr)
 \tag{4.2}
\]

among all positive-positive nonzero secants.  Put

\[
 u=\Phi(S_0)-\Phi(S_1)
 =\sum_{t\in S_0\setminus S_1}v_t
  -\sum_{t\in S_1\setminus S_0}v_t,
 \tag{4.3}
\]

\[
 K=\langle u\rangle\le G,
 \qquad d=|K|=d_*.
 \tag{4.4}
\]

This is an explicit source secant rooted at the two actual positive-weight
owners `o(S_0),o(S_1)`.

Let

\[
 r:\widehat G\to\widehat K,
 \qquad r(\gamma)=\gamma|_K.
\]

Its fibers are cosets of

\[
 K^\perp=\{\gamma\in\widehat G:\gamma(k)=1\text{ for all }k\in K\},
\]

and

\[
 [\widehat G:K^\perp]=|K|=d.
 \tag{4.5}
\]

For `chi in K-hat`, set

\[
 A_\chi=A\cap r^{-1}(\chi),
 \qquad
 h_\chi=P_{A_\chi}b,
 \qquad
 E_\chi=\|h_\chi\|_2^2,
 \qquad
 m_\chi=|A_\chi|.
 \tag{4.6}
\]

There are at most `d` nonempty classes and

\[
 P_Ab=\sum_\chi h_\chi,
 \qquad
 E_A(b)=\sum_\chi E_\chi,
 \qquad
 |A|=\sum_\chi m_\chi.
 \tag{4.7}
\]

Choose classes `chi_q, chi_E, chi_m` such that

\[
 \|h_{\chi_q}\|_q\ge\frac1d\|P_Ab\|_q,
 \qquad
 E_{\chi_E}\ge\frac1dE_A(b),
 \qquad
 m_{\chi_m}\ge\frac1d|A|.
 \tag{4.8}
\]

The first inequality is Minkowski; the other two are averaging.

Let `J` consist of these three restrictions and their negatives, and put

\[
 A'=\bigcup_{\chi\in J}A_\chi.
 \tag{4.9}
\]

Because `A=-A`, the packet `A'` is symmetric, is contained in `A`, and lies
in at most six cosets of `K^perp`.

### 4.1 The same packet retains the q-norm

For `chi in K-hat`, define

\[
 Q_\chi h(s)
 =\frac1d\sum_{k\in K}\overline{\chi(k)}h(s+k).
 \tag{4.10}
\]

Every translation is an `ell^q` isometry, so

\[
 \|Q_\chi h\|_q\le\|h\|_q.
 \tag{4.11}
\]

Fourier orthogonality on `K` shows that `Q_chi` is exactly the spectral
projection onto characters restricting to `chi`.  Since `A'` contains the
whole class `A_{chi_q}`,

\[
 Q_{\chi_q}P_{A'}b=P_{A_{\chi_q}}b=h_{\chi_q}.
\]

Therefore

\[
 \boxed{
 \|P_{A'}b\|_q
 \ge\|h_{\chi_q}\|_q
 \ge\frac1d\|P_Ab\|_q.
 }
 \tag{4.12}
\]

In particular,

\[
 \boxed{
 \mathcal R_{A'}(b)
 \ge\frac1d\mathcal R_A(b)
 \ge\frac1d\mathcal R_A(f).
 }
 \tag{4.13}
\]

### 4.2 The same packet retains projected energy and density

By construction,

\[
 E_{A'}(b)\ge\frac1dE_A(b),
 \qquad
 |A'|\ge\frac1d|A|.
 \tag{4.14}
\]

Consequently

\[
 e_{A'}(b)\ge\frac1d e_A(b),
 \qquad
 x_{A'}(b)\ge\frac1d x_A(b),
\]

and hence

\[
 \boxed{
 \mathcal Y_{A'}(b)
 \ge\frac1{d^{q-1}}\mathcal Y_A(b).
 }
 \tag{4.15}
\]

Thus one and the same symmetric packet retains the q-excess, Fourier energy,
and character density.  No cancellation issue remains after adjoining the
other heavy classes, because the contractive projector (4.10) recovers the
q-heavy component from their union.

There is no support, owner, or positive-mass loss:

The structural datum is

Every entry is finite and verifier-checkable.  It prints the source equation

## 5. Flat rooted source inverse theorem

### Theorem 5.1 (secant-annihilator localization with exact order loss)

Under (1.1)--(1.3), exactly one of the following explicit precursor outputs
is produced.

1. If `Phi(S_+)` is a singleton, Sections 2--3 produce a fixed-radius family
   of at least `exp(2 eta N-o(N))` exact planted trades, carrying at least an
   `1/(2N)` fraction of the positive dual mass and rooted at actual surviving
   owners.  The full packet retains all supports, owners, characters, mass,
   q-excess, and projected load.
2. If `Phi(S_+)` has at least two points, Section 4 produces a nonzero
   positive-rooted source secant `u`, the cyclic subgroup `K=<u>`, and one
   symmetric packet `A'` contained in at most six cosets of `K^perp`, with

   \[
   [\widehat G:K^\perp]=d_*,
   \qquad |A'|\ge |A|/d_*,
   \tag{5.1}
   \]

   \[
   \mathcal R_{A'}(b)\ge \mathcal R_A(f)/d_*,
   \qquad
   \mathcal Y_{A'}(b)\ge \mathcal Y_A(b)/d_*^{q-1}.
   \tag{5.2}
   \]

   Every positive support, actual owner, and unit of positive dual mass is
   retained.

This theorem uses neither primitive Q, A4, complete-band restriction bound, C9, a max-fiber theorem, nor
semantic-atlas payment.

### Compiler-grade corollary

If

\[
 \log d_*=o(N)
 \tag{5.3}
\]

uniformly, then (5.1)--(5.2) have exactly the requested losses:

\[
 |A'|\ge e^{-o(N)}|A|,
 \qquad
 \mathcal R_{A'}(b)\ge e^{\eta N-o(N)},
\]

\[
 \mathcal Y_{A'}(b)\ge e^{\eta Nq-o(Nq)}.
 \tag{5.4}
\]

The support, owner, and positive-mass losses are one.

For a source boundary group contained in the additive group of
`B^R`, every nonzero element has order

\[
 p=\operatorname{char}B.
\]

Hence `d_*=p`.  In particular, (5.3) is automatic whenever

\[
 \log|B|=o(N),
 \tag{5.5}
\]

including polynomial-size coefficient fields.

## 6. Why the secant-order parameter must be printed

The hypotheses `log L/q=o(N)` and `L<=M<=2^N` do not bound the exponent of
the ambient/effective additive group.  A realized image of at most `2^N`
points may lie in an additive group of prime characteristic much larger than
`e^N`.  Therefore (5.3) does not follow from the displayed flat rooted source inverse hypotheses
alone.

The exact finite theorem remains valid with the printed loss `d_*`.  A fully
uniform statement must do one of the following:

1. include `log char(B)=o(N)` (or the sharper (5.3)) among source
   accessibility assumptions;
2. permit a rooted large-order source secant as an additional precursor; or
3. prove a separate large-characteristic source inverse theorem.

No `o(N)` term may silently absorb `log d_*`.

## 7. Route audit and semantic-atlas boundary

* The raw collision route closes the same-syndrome branch and gives the
  planted family in Section 3, but the flat branch may have only diagonal
  physical energy.
* The individual witness-evaluation rank matrix is deficient for every
  decoding witness and is therefore a baseline identity, not new collective
  structure.
* Quadratic-Bohr trapping is approximate and does not by itself print an
  exact quotient, trade, proper field, or collective deficient matrix.
* Frequency popularity alone gives separate q-heavy, energy-heavy, and
  size-heavy restriction classes.  The contractive subgroup projector is the
  missing device that joins them into the single packet (4.9) without losing
  the q-norm.

The output is a literal owner-rooted quotient precursor.  It is not yet a canonical
atlas quotient: the semantic atlas conversion must decide whether the cyclic annihilator is a
recognized folding/trace/field/rank cell and prove its distinct-slope budget.
If the semantic atlas conversion intends “quotient precursor” to mean only a pre-existing algebraic
folding, that stronger semantic requirement must be added to the flat rooted source inverse
statement; it is not a consequence of the present quotient syntax.
